Key Findings

The early warning system correctly flagged 2,652 out of 3,185 at-risk school-subgroups on unseen 2025 LEAP data, achieving 83.3% operational recall.

English Learners face the highest risk at 91.7%. Students with Disabilities are at 72.9%.

Black students are 3.7x more likely to be at-risk than White students (32.0% vs 8.6%).

ELA Mastery rate is the strongest predictor (SHAP = 1.21).

What this means for educators: Instead of waiting until summer to find out which students struggled, this system tells you at the START of the school year which groups need help. Each prediction comes with plain-language reasons why.

What This Means for Educators (Plain Language)

ELA Mastery Rate is the single most important factor (SHAP = 1.21). Schools where fewer students reach Mastery in English Language Arts are most likely to be flagged.

School Performance Score (SHAP = 0.60) captures overall school quality. A declining SPS signals trouble even if individual subject scores look okay.

Subgroup identity + minority + disadvantaged flags (combined SHAP = 0.69) confirm that demographic factors compound academic risk.

Example: What a Teacher Would See

"Students with Disabilities at Southside Elementary (East Carroll Parish) are flagged as HIGH RISK. Top factors: ELA mastery rate is very low (50% unsatisfactory), the school's SPS is 29 (state average is 75), and East Carroll Parish has the highest poverty rate in Louisiana. Recommended action: Prioritize this subgroup for immediate reading intervention and additional special education support."
